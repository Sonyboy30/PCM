Legacy & Significance

Opened in 1914, the canal reconfigured maritime routes, accelerated trade, and became a symbol of engineering prowess—alongside enduring debates over power and equity. Modern expansions and water-management challenges continue to shape its future.

“The canal was not just a triumph of engineering—it was a statement of power.”
