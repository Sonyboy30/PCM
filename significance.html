Why the Canal Still Matters

The canal re-mapped global trade in 1914 and continues to shape shipping routes and supply chains. Transfer to Panamanian control (1999) and later expansions underscore its evolving role as both national asset and global utility.

Progressive Era Takeaways

  • State capacity: coordination of engineering, medicine, logistics, and labor at scale.
  • Public health: mosquito control as a model for preventive infrastructure.
  • Power & principle: tension between modernization and imperial reach.
