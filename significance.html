Legacy & Significance

Opened in 1914, the Panama Canal remains a vital artery for world commerce and a touchstone of twentieth-century history.

Enduring Impact

Recognized among the “wonders of the modern world,” the Canal continues to shorten sea routes and anchor port economies. Modern expansions and environmental challenges keep its story current.

“The canal was not just a triumph of engineering — it was a statement of power.”

Memory & Reflection

The project’s legacy includes hard questions about labor systems, sovereignty, and public health — lessons that still shape large infrastructure projects today.
