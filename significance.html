Legacy & Significance

The Panama Canal was a hallmark of Progressive Era state capacity. It joined public health, engineering, and administration to deliver a global trade shortcut. It also raised questions about consent and control that continued into the twentieth century and beyond.

Why It Mattered in the Progressive Era

  • State capacity: coordinating engineering, sanitation, and logistics at national scale.
  • Science and health: vector control as public policy.
  • Administration: complex governance for locks, lake, dams, and labor.
  • Power and diplomacy: the Canal signaled a stronger American role in the region.
