Legacy & Significance

Completed in 1914, the Panama Canal revolutionized global trade. Ships could now travel between oceans in half the time, accelerating commerce and connecting economies.

The canal stands as a hallmark of Progressive Era ideals—innovation, efficiency, and control—while also reflecting the contradictions of U.S. imperialism and global dominance.

Today, the canal remains vital, handling over 5% of global maritime trade and representing human ingenuity’s power to reshape the planet.
