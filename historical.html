Historical Background

The idea of a water passage through Panama dates to the sixteenth century. In the late nineteenth century, French engineer Ferdinand de Lesseps began a sea-level project that stalled after landslides, disease, and financial collapse. In 1903, the United States supported Panamanian independence and negotiated the right to build. The American plan embraced a lock system suited to the terrain.
