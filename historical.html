Historical Background

The dream of linking the Atlantic and Pacific through Panama dates to the 1500s. In the 1880s, French engineer Ferdinand de Lesseps—builder of the Suez Canal—attempted a sea-level canal, but disease, landslides, and financial collapse ended the effort.

In 1903, the United States supported Panama’s independence from Colombia and secured rights to build. Shifting to a lock-based design, construction formally began in 1904 and culminated in the canal’s 1914 opening—reshaping world trade and projecting U.S. power.
