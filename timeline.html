Timeline

Key moments from vision to opening, paraphrased from PBS’s “Creating the Canal” timeline and standard references.

1869

Suez Canal completed; U.S. interest in an isthmian canal grows

French success in Egypt inspires surveys in Central America and fuels the idea of a cross-isthmus route.

1880s

French sea-level attempt begins and fails

Floods, landslides, and disease overwhelm the project; finances collapse and work ends by 1889.

1903

Panama becomes independent; treaty grants U.S. rights to build

The Hay-Bunau-Varilla agreement provides a zone to construct and operate the waterway.

1904–1906

American reorganization and sanitation

John Stevens revamps rail haulage; William Gorgas reduces yellow fever and malaria through vector control.

1907–1911

Goethals leads heavy excavation and lock works

Culebra Cut advances; Gatun Dam and the lock complexes rise as core structures.

1913

Waterway connected

The channel reaches through; the system nears operational readiness.

1914

Official opening

SS Ancon makes the first official transit on August 15, 1914.
