Panama Canal Timeline (1869–1914)

Key milestones from early ambitions to the Canal’s opening—drawn from the PBS American Experience timeline and complementary sources.

1869

Suez Canal completed (De Lesseps); US interest in an isthmian route grows.

1875

US survey in Panama deems a canal costly; Nicaragua considered.

1879–1880

French begin a sea-level canal in Panama led by Ferdinand de Lesseps.

1883–1884

Disease & landslides cripple French effort; deaths surge from yellow fever/malaria.

1888

French company collapses after massive losses; project stalls.

1903

Panama independence & Hay–Bunau-Varilla Treaty grant US canal rights.

1904

US construction begins; early setbacks with disease and equipment.

1905

John Stevens reorganizes works; Dr. William Gorgas expands mosquito control.

1906

President Roosevelt visits the canal zone—first sitting US president abroad.

1907

Goethals takes command; excavation surges; lock-canal design locked in.

1909–1911

Locks & dams rise at Gatún, Pedro Miguel, and Miraflores; workforce peaks.

1913

Culebra Cut breakthrough; Gatún Dam closed to fill lake.

1914

Canal opens (Aug 15); SS Ancon makes first official transit.

Sources: PBS American Experience timeline; Office of the Historian (U.S. Dept. of State); HISTORY overview.
