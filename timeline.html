1869

Suez Completed; U.S. Surveys Central America

Ferdinand de Lesseps finishes Suez; U.S. commissions early canal route studies.

1879–1888

French Attempt in Panama

Sea-level plan falters amid landslides, yellow fever, and bankruptcy.

1903

Panama Independence & Canal Treaty

U.S. recognizes Panama; Hay–Bunau-Varilla Treaty grants canal rights.

1904–1906

Start of U.S. Effort; Public Health Offensive

John Wallace then John Stevens lead; Dr. Gorgas’ mosquito control ends yellow fever by late 1905.

1907–1912

Goethals; Culebra Cut & Locks

Round-the-clock excavation and concrete locks at Gatún, Pedro Miguel, Miraflores.

1913

Waterway Connected

Final dike blasts; continuous channel from ocean to ocean.

Aug 15, 1914

Canal Opens

SS Ancon transits; WWI mutes celebrations.

1977 → 1999

Treaties & Handover

Torrijos–Carter agreements lead to full Panamanian control in 1999.
