Timeline

Key moments from early surveys to opening day. Drag, scroll, or use arrow keys.

1869

Interest in an inter-ocean route continues as global trade expands.

1879

French sea-level plan approved; work begins but faces disease and landslides.

1888–1893

French company collapses; investigations follow in France.

1903

U.S. backs a canal project after diplomatic changes in Panama.

1904

American phase begins; sanitation and logistics scale up.

1905–1907

Public health advances; shift to a lock canal is adopted.

1909–1911

Lock construction and dam works accelerate.

1913

Major excavation milestones reached; channels connected.

1914

Opening to traffic marks completion of the project.
