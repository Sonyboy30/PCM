In Honor of Those Who Built the Canal

This exhibit honors the tens of thousands of workers, engineers, nurses, and families who transformed the isthmus into a working waterway between two oceans. Their labor—across languages and nationalities— faced landslides, heat, and disease. Guided by new public-health science and bold engineering, they forged locks, dams, and a lake that reshaped trade and diplomacy. The Canal’s story reflects the Progressive Era’s faith in expertise and infrastructure, as well as the hard truths of inequality and sovereignty that marked its creation. May their perseverance and skill continue to inspire future builders and citizens.
