Social & Political Impact

The Canal Zone reflected global labor flows and hierarchies: recruitment from the Caribbean and Latin America, tiered pay and housing, and a rigid “gold/silver” system that shaped daily life.

Politically, the canal anchored U.S. influence for decades. The Torrijos-Carter Treaties (1977) began the transfer of control, completed in 1999, reframing sovereignty and U.S.–Panama relations.
