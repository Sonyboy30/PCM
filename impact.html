Social & Political Impact

The Canal intertwined engineering with labor and sovereignty. The workforce included thousands from the Caribbean and Latin America who faced hard conditions and a tiered pay system. Politically, the project signaled a stronger American role in the hemisphere and reshaped U.S.–Panama relations.
