Welcome

The Panama Canal stands as one of humanity’s most impressive engineering feats. Explore how ambition, innovation, public health, labor, and geopolitics shaped its creation and lasting impact on global trade.

Built in the early 1900s, the Canal reshaped maritime routes by linking the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. Its story is one of determination, disease control, politics, segregation, and progress — a symbol of both human ingenuity and imperial ambition.
