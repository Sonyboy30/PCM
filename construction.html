Construction & Challenges

Work began in 1904 with Chief Engineer John Wallace, then John Stevens, and later George Washington Goethals. The redesign to a lock canal allowed ships to be raised to Gatun Lake and lowered to sea level on each side. Sanitary officer William Gorgas led mosquito control that reduced yellow fever and malaria.

Artifacts

  • Steam shovel bucket — symbol of mass earth-moving in the Cut.
  • Lock control panel sketch — shows electric operation of massive gates.
  • Sanitation crew spray canister — represents public health engineering.
