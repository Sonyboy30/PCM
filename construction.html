Construction & Challenges

From redesigning the canal to conquering disease and landslides, the build tested every facet of engineering and public health.

Design & Engineering

After the French failure, the U.S. adopted a lock-based system to raise ships to Gatún Lake and down again, overcoming elevation and controlling the Chagres River. Chief Engineers John Stevens and later George Washington Goethals coordinated a vast operation of railroads, dams, and concrete.

Health & Safety

Malaria and yellow fever initially devastated the workforce. Under Dr. William Gorgas, sanitation campaigns drained standing water, screened buildings, and fumigated — dramatically reducing disease.

Earthworks & Risk

The Culebra Cut required relentless excavation and constant rebuilding after landslides. Thousands of workers labored in intense heat around explosives, steam shovels, and trains.
