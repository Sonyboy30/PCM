Engineering a Lock Canal

American engineers abandoned the French sea-level idea and built a lock canal that lifts ships ~85 feet to Gatún Lake. Excavation concentrated on Culebra Cut while massive dams (Gatún, Miraflores) and paired concrete locks enabled gravity-fed lifts and reliable control of the Chagres River.

Public Health as Infrastructure

Under Dr. William Gorgas, crews drained standing water, screened buildings, and fumigated to kill mosquitoes. Cases of yellow fever disappeared by late 1905, and malaria dropped dramatically—turning an unworkable site into a manageable one.

By the Numbers

  • Hundreds of millions of cubic yards excavated during the U.S. phase.
  • Locks: ~110 ft wide, ~1,000 ft long chambers; electric control systems for gates and culverts.
  • Completion milestone: August 15, 1914 (SS Ancon transit).
