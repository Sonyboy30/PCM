Construction & Challenges

Locks, dams, disease control, landslides, and daily risk in a tropical worksite.

American engineers adopted a lock system to raise ships to Gatun Lake and lower them back to sea level. This choice reduced excavation and improved control of the Chagres River. At Culebra Cut, slides were frequent, and work trains hauled spoil day and night.

Disease control was central. Guided by public health science, teams drained standing water, screened buildings, fumigated neighborhoods, and provided sanitation services to cut malaria and yellow fever. These efforts mirrored Progressive Era confidence in expertise and public health campaigns.

“The canal presents a problem not of mere digging, but of organization, health, and discipline.”

— Contemporary observation on canal management
