Construction & Challenges

The American phase began in 1904 under John Stevens, later George W. Goethals. Abandoning a sea-level plan, engineers built paired locks lifting ships to Gatún Lake. Dr. William Gorgas’s sanitation campaign targeted mosquitoes, slashing yellow fever and malaria.

Crews faced landslides at Culebra Cut, dynamite risks, heavy tropical rain, and extreme heat. By 1913, hundreds of millions of cubic yards had been moved; locks, dams, railways, and power systems were integrated into a coordinated whole.
